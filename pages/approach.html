I. Approach to Testing and Quality Assurance

There’s an adage that “A project isn’t successful just because it has been completed on time and within budget. There is one other factor that is critical to its success: Quality” .

You_Source acknowledges the importance of Quality Assurance in Software Testing and likewise believes that true quality comes from quality minded people.

Quality Assurance Division’s approach to testing and quality assurance encapsulates the processes into:

REQUIREMENTS ANALYSIS

The Shift-Left Approach is a proactive way in Quality Assurance. Involvement of the QA as early as during the Sprint Planning, provides a guarantee that the product will work without any problems as per the expectations or requirements.

TEST DESIGN

While the Developers are working on their coding tasks, the QA team is preparing the Test Plans, Test Cases and Test Scenarios to ensure that test coverage is examined carefully to accomplish genuine quality.

TEST EXECUTION

As an integral part of both the software development and software testing life cycle, test execution involves the comparison of the expected and actual results. Test suites of execution records are artifacts that are being assessed on a timely basis.

DEFECT MANAGEMENT

The goal of Defect Management is to “provide information to improve the development process”. A defect management cycle contains the following stages:

  1. Discovery of Defect
  2. Defect Categorization
  3. Fixing of Defect by developers
  4. Verification by Testers
  5. Defect Closure
  6. Defect Reports at the end of the project

III. Test Case Management Process and Tools

Test case management is the process of managing testing activities to ensure high-quality and end-to-end testing of software applications.

Depending on the project, our QAs either writes the Test Cases on Excel and/or directly in the Test Plan repository of Azure DevOps. The advantage of the latter is that QA can create test plans and test suites to track manual testing for sprints or milestone, and it provides the detail as to when the testing for a specific sprint or milestone is complete.

IV. Backlog Management

  • Acceptance Tests to define the acceptance criteria for the functionality
  • Tasks to define discrete work units for the team to complete to deliver the story.
